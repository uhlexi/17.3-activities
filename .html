Forest Preservation

Forest preservation is crucial for maintaining biodiversity, regulating climate, and sustaining the livelihoods of millions of people. Forests act as carbon sinks, absorbing carbon dioxide and mitigating the effects of climate change. They provide habitats for countless species, ensuring the survival of flora and fauna that rely on these ecosystems. Additionally, forests play a vital role in the water cycle, preventing soil erosion and maintaining water quality. Efforts to preserve forests include establishing protected areas, promoting sustainable forestry practices, and combating illegal logging. By preserving forests, we ensure a healthier planet for future generations.
